A high-energy, hands-on 2-hour session built for JITO youth members running or entering family businesses across mixed industries. Curriculum

Module-by-Module Breakdown

Three tightly structured modules, each 40 minutes. Built to move fast, stay practical, and leave every participant with something they can use the same week.

01
Foundation
AI Literacy. What It Is, What It Is Not, and Why It Matters Now
Objective
Every participant understands what AI actually is, how it fits their business, and why the window to act early is right now.
Topics Covered
  • What AI is and what it is not. Kill the sci-fi myths in the first 5 minutes.
  • How family businesses in retail, manufacturing, and services are already using AI today.
  • The shift from AI as a tool to AI as a team member who never sleeps.
  • ChatGPT vs Claude vs Gemini. Which one to actually use and why.
  • The only mindset shift that matters. Stop typing queries. Start giving assignments.
Outcome
Participants understand the real-world stakes and can correctly identify which AI tool fits their business type.
02
Core Skill
Prompt Engineering. The Skill Nobody Taught You
Objective
Participants move from vague one-liners to structured prompts that get finished, usable work on the first try.
Topics Covered
  • Why bad prompts waste time and how to spot them instantly.
  • The RCTF framework: Role, Context, Task, Format. One structure that works across every use case.
  • Live demo: Same task, 3 different prompts, 3 wildly different outputs. The room decides the winner.
  • Business prompts for your world: vendor emails, staff briefings, social media captions, sales pitches.
  • The one-shot vs multi-shot approach. When to refine vs when to restart.
Outcome
Participants can write a structured business prompt independently and get a ready-to-use output within two tries.
03
Application
GenAI for Your Business. Real Use Cases, Not Theory
Objective
Every participant walks away with AI use cases they can test in their own business before the week ends, plus a live look at how automation works.
Topics Covered
  • Content and marketing: product descriptions, Instagram captions, WhatsApp broadcast messages, and sales pitches built in seconds.
  • Operations and sales: SOP drafting, vendor comparison emails, follow-up templates, and objection handling scripts.
  • Common mistakes to avoid: hallucinations, the copy-paste trap, privacy risks, and a 60-second review habit that prevents 90 percent of errors.
  • Rapid-fire use case challenge: someone from the audience shouts a real business problem and Rishi builds the output live in 90 seconds.
Outcome
Participants identify their top time-draining tasks, understand how to apply AI immediately, and see a working automation example mapped to a real business.
